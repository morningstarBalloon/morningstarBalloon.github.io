Welcome to
Captain-Crystal's

Morning Star Balloon Co.

Morning Star Balloon Co. enjoys traveling to near and far-away places to share their love for ballooning. Passenger flights are conducted in the Olympic Peninsula, out of the Sequim Valley Airport. Enjoy magnificent views of the Olympic National Forest, the Olympic Mountains (including Hurricane Ridge and Mt. Olympus), the Puget Sound, Victoria (British Columbia), the Cascade Range, Protection Island, and the Dungeness Area.

The “Blue Hole’ of the Olympics produces only 16” of rain annually, which makes for sunny, flyable mornings year round. Take flight, today!

About Us

Get to know the captain and crew of the Morning Star

Captain Crystal Stout

Captain Crystal Stout brings a joy to ballooning with her expert skill as a pilot and her experiences as a story teller. She enjoys thinking outside of the box to make your special occasion that much more enjoyable. She is a 3rd Generation hot air balloonist who has had the pleasure of flying in many parts of the world.

The Dream Team

The Morning Star Balloon Team (our Crew) are a fun and enthusiastic addition to the flight experience. Every year is an exciting year for us. Why? Because we get to fly balloons, meet new people, explore new skies, share wonderful memories, and teach adults to play like children!

Captain-Crystal, Chief Flight Officer and Owner of Morning Star Balloon Co., has been flying hot air balloons for over 27 years. Known as the “Peter Pan” for adults, her true passion is to teach adults to play like children. She knows a smart pilot seeks ways to get the business she is promoting into the spotlight and goes far beyond the 'norm' to ensure her clients get exposure.

An experienced aviatrix, she has delved into powered parachutes, airplanes, and helicopters, but loves the challenge and joy of sharing hot air ballooning with people all over the planet. As a Champion Corporate Hot Air Balloon Pilot, Captain Crystal traveled the globe and created events in New Zealand, Puerto Rico, Japan, and all across the United States. As the Chief Pilot of Morning Star Balloon Co., Captain Crystal has won numerous hot air balloon competitions and has ranked in the Top 10 Women Lighter Than Air Pilots in the U.S. She is also a 3rd Generation aviator, following in the footsteps of her grandfather who crewed on the original Stratospheric Balloon; launched in 1935. Her grandfather taught her father to pilot a hot air balloon as well. Before becoming a confident and enthusiastic competitor, she completed schooling at one of three 141 Aviation Schools in the United States. Fierce educational values were in place at the school and the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) used this Oregon-based school as a test facility, enabling Captain Crystal to undergo duplicate flight training.

With a Degree in Radio and Television Broadcasting, Captain Crystal has the marketing edge and knows how to speak to the media for her corporate accounts. While working as the Spokesperson for PPG/CART (Indy Car Racing Teams) and the KOOL Balloon Racing Series, she traveled to 10 cities across the U.S. and was interviewed by Good Morning America, Good Morning Detroit, Miami, St. Louis, Good Morning Monterey, and other Networks and Affiliates. While in New Zealand, she was the Media Liaison and Co-Balloonmeister (or person in charge of pilots, competitions, sponsorships, and marketing). A normal day required 19 hours of time on the road conducting balloon flights, balloon tethers, radio and print interviews, television interviews, and arrangements for pilots, crew, and sponsors.

Captain Crystal has flown the largest balloon in the world! This was a special shaped Indy racecar which stood a staggering 18 stories tall. She has flown in extreme conditions for Absolut Mandrin and Seagrams Vodka, during a wind storm in Chicago, and a hurricane front in Miami. With a safety record that spans her flight career and dedication to getting the job done, she was able to keep the balloon aloft and “on air” for the helicopters circling above and completed a 4 city tour across the U.S. for the launch of this new Swedish product.

Her latest endeavor is to produce a special, two person balloon which sits on “legs” (instead of a basket) for special needs, wounded warriors, other-abled, and educational purposes. Known as the Dream Catcher Balloon Program, this non-profit corporation will fill the skies and bring joy to those in wheelchairs, held down to earth by physical or mental conditions, and educate the world that we can all help those around us rise to greater heights.
